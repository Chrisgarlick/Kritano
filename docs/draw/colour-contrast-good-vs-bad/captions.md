## Slide 2 — Light Grey on White

**Caption:**
Light grey on white — the most common contrast failure on the web.

#CBD5E1 on white = 1.9:1 ratio. Fail.
#475569 on white = 7.0:1 ratio. Pass.

WCAG AA requires 4.5:1 for body text. Most "subtle" grey text doesn't even come close.

## Slide 3 — Yellow on White

**Caption:**
Yellow on white. Ratio: 1.7:1. That's an instant fail.

Amber, yellow, and orange text only work on dark backgrounds. On white, you need amber-800 (#92400E) or darker to pass WCAG AA.

If your sale banner uses yellow text on a light background, your biggest sale is invisible to millions.

## Slide 4 — Blue on Blue

**Caption:**
Indigo-400 on indigo-600. It looks fine to you. It's a 2.4:1 fail.

White text on the same background? 8.6:1. Crystal clear.

Never use a lighter shade of the same colour for text on a coloured background. It looks "designed" but fails every time.

## Slide 5 — Green on Dark

**Caption:**
Green on dark backgrounds. The "success" colour that often fails.

emerald-500 (#10B981) on slate-900 = 2.8:1. Fail.
emerald-300 (#6EE7B7) on slate-900 = 7.3:1. Pass.

Use the 300 shade on dark backgrounds. Still reads as success. Actually readable.

## Slide 6 — The WCAG Rules

**Caption:**
WCAG contrast ratios — the cheat sheet.

AA (legal minimum):
→ 4.5:1 for normal text
→ 3:1 for large text (18pt+)
→ 3:1 for UI components

AAA (gold standard):
→ 7:1 for normal text
→ 4.5:1 for large text

AA is the law. AAA is the goal. Aim for AA minimum on everything.
