## Slide 2 — Missing Alt Text

**Caption:**
Issue 1: Missing alt text.

Screen readers can't describe your images without it. Search engines can't index them either. Every meaningful image needs a real description — and every decorative image needs an empty alt attribute.

Quick fix: Audit every img tag. Describe content images. Use alt="" for decorative ones.

## Slide 3 — Poor Colour Contrast

**Caption:**
Issue 2: Poor colour contrast.

If your text doesn't hit a 4.5:1 contrast ratio, it's unreadable for users with visual impairments — and annoying for everyone else in bright sunlight.

Quick fix: Test every text/background combination against WCAG AA. It takes five minutes and makes a massive difference.

## Slide 4 — No Heading Hierarchy

**Caption:**
Issue 3: No heading hierarchy.

Jumping from h1 to h4 breaks your document outline. Screen readers use headings to navigate — skipping levels is like ripping chapters out of a book.

Quick fix: One h1 per page, then nest h2 → h3 → h4 sequentially. Never skip levels.

## Slide 5 — Broken Links

**Caption:**
Issue 4: Broken links.

Dead links frustrate users, waste crawl budget, and silently tank your search rankings. They accumulate over time — and 88% of users won't come back after a bad experience.

Quick fix: Run a crawl, set up redirects for moved pages, and add a helpful custom 404 page.

## Slide 6 — Missing Form Labels

**Caption:**
Issue 5: Missing form labels.

Forms without proper label elements are unusable with screen readers. Placeholder text alone isn't enough — it disappears the moment someone starts typing.

Quick fix: Add a label for every input. Keep placeholders as hints, not replacements. Always use visible labels.
