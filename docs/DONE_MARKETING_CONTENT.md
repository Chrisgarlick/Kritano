# PagePulser — 3-Month Marketing Content Plan

## Overview

A full 90-day content calendar covering **pre-launch** (Month 1), **launch** (Month 2), and **post-launch / growth** (Month 3). All content centres on PagePulser's core value prop: helping businesses understand their website's accessibility, SEO, security, and performance health.

### Daily / Weekly Cadence

| Platform | Frequency | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Twitter/X | 1 post per day (7/week) | Short-form tips, stats, engagement |
| Instagram | 1 post per day (7/week) | Carousels, infographics, reels scripts |
| Threads | 1 post per day (7/week) | Conversational, community-driven |
| LinkedIn | 2 posts per week | Long-form thought leadership |
| Blog | 2 posts per week | SEO-driven articles, guides, case studies |

### Content Pillars

1. **Education** — Accessibility, SEO, security, and performance tips
2. **Product** — Features, demos, behind-the-scenes
3. **Social Proof** — Stats, testimonials, case studies
4. **Community** — Engagement, questions, discussions
5. **Industry** — News, trends, regulatory updates (WCAG, EAA, ADA)

### Tone

Authoritative but approachable. Data-driven. Helpful. Never preachy. Always action-oriented — "here's what you can do."

**Post 2 (Fri):**
> **The European Accessibility Act: A Simple Guide for UK Businesses**
>
> The European Accessibility Act (EAA) comes into force on 28 June 2025. If you sell products or services to EU customers — yes, even from the UK — this affects you.
>
> Here's what you need to know, without the legal jargon:
>
> **Who it applies to:**
> Any business selling to EU consumers with a digital presence. E-commerce, SaaS, banking, transport, media.
>
> **What it requires:**
> Your website must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards. That means perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for all users, including those with disabilities.
>
> **What happens if you don't comply:**
> Each EU member state sets its own penalties, but expect fines, market access restrictions, and potential lawsuits.
>
> **What you should do now:**
> 1. Audit your website against WCAG 2.1 AA
> 2. Fix critical issues (alt text, contrast, keyboard nav, form labels)
> 3. Set up regular monitoring — compliance isn't one-and-done
> 4. Document your efforts (shows good faith)
>
> We're building PagePulser to make step 1 and 3 effortless. Automated audits, clear reports, actionable guidance.
>
> Don't wait until June. Start now.
