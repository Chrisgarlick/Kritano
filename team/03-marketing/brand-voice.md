# Kritano Brand Voice Guide

> The canonical voice reference for all Kritano content. Replaces the earlier `docs/marketing/brand_voice.md` (a personal voice analysis of chrisgarlick.com) with a guide built specifically for the Kritano brand.

---

## 1. Voice Overview

**One-line summary:** Authoritative, clear, and genuinely helpful — like a trusted consultant who explains the problem, tells you exactly how to fix it, and never makes you feel stupid for not knowing.

**Brand archetype:** The Sage — wisdom, insight, and expertise — with an Explorer's curiosity for revealing what's hidden.

**Founder influence:** Kritano's voice inherits its conversational accessibility and honest opinions from founder Chris Garlick's personal writing style, but channels them through a more precise, data-driven SaaS lens. The warmth stays; the chattiness is tightened.

---

## 2. Core Voice Attributes

### 2.1 Authoritative Without Arrogance
We know our craft. We state things directly and back claims with evidence. We never lecture, condescend, or use authority as a substitute for clarity.

**Kritano examples:**
- "The clarity your website deserves."
- "We'd rather show you 10 real issues than 100 false positives."
- "It flags the issues that actually affect your rankings, not vanity metrics that don't move the needle."

### 2.2 Clear Over Clever
No jargon soup. Technical concepts are always made accessible. If we use a technical term, we explain it immediately. We favour precision over flair — every word should earn its place.

**Kritano examples:**
- "Kritano measures your Core Web Vitals — LCP, INP, and CLS — alongside other performance metrics."
- "We translate technical findings into plain language with clear next steps. No jargon, no confusion."
- "You don't need to be a developer to understand the results — that was a non-negotiable from day one."

### 2.3 Helpful, Not Just Critical
Findings always come with guidance. We don't just point out problems — we explain how to fix them and why it matters. Our tone is constructive, never alarmist.

**Kritano examples:**
- "The number isn't a judgement — it's a starting point."
- "Most websites I've audited start somewhere between 40 and 60. That's normal."
- "The free tier tells you *what's wrong*. The paid tiers tell you *how to fix it*."

### 2.4 Honest and Direct
We acknowledge trade-offs openly. We don't oversell or create artificial urgency. When something has a downside, we say so. This earns trust.

**Kritano examples:**
- "Don't be discouraged if your initial scores are lower than you expected."
- "For many smaller sites, that might be all you need."
- "Here's the honest take..."

### 2.5 Forward-Thinking
We help people see what's coming, not just what's broken. We position Kritano at the frontier — covering AI readiness and emerging standards alongside established best practices.

**Kritano examples:**
- "How likely are AI models and answer engines to cite your pages? This is a newer dimension that most audit tools don't touch."
- "The web evolves, and so do we."

## 3. Voice by Content Type

| Attribute | Website Copy | Blog Posts | Product UI | Email |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Formality** | Medium | Medium-low | Medium-high | Medium |
| **Person** | 1st plural ("we") | 1st singular ("I") or plural | 2nd person ("you") | 1st plural / 2nd |
| **Tone** | Confident, editorial | Conversational, educational | Precise, supportive | Warm, action-oriented |
| **Purpose** | Attract & orient | Educate & build trust | Guide & inform | Engage & convert |
| **CTA style** | Direct but inviting | Soft, value-led | Contextual | Clear and specific |
| **Sentence length** | Short to medium | Mixed (short for emphasis, longer for explanation) | Short and scannable | Short |

### Point of View Rules
- **Public website pages** (Home, About, Services, Pricing): First person plural ("we", "our") — Kritano is the speaker.
- **Blog posts**: First person singular ("I") when sharing opinion or experience (the founder's voice); first person plural when describing the product. This is intentional — the blog carries the human voice behind the brand.
- **Product UI**: Second person ("you", "your") — direct and action-oriented.
- **Email**: First person plural with second person address ("We noticed your scores improved — here's what changed").

## 4. Writing Style

### 4.1 Sentence Structure
- Mix short and long sentences. Short for impact, long for explanation.
- Use em dashes (—) for asides and clarifications, not parentheses.
- Rhetorical questions are welcome in blog openings and hero copy, but use sparingly in product UI.
- Avoid exclamation marks in product UI and website copy. Reserve for celebrations (e.g. achievement emails) only.

### 4.2 Paragraph Structure
- Blog posts: 2-4 sentences per paragraph. Never walls of text.
- Website copy: 1-3 sentences. Tight and scannable.
- Product UI: Single sentences or bullet points.

### 4.3 Vocabulary
- **Reading level:** Accessible to non-technical business owners. Roughly UK secondary school level.
- **Technical terms:** Used when accurate, always explained immediately.
- **British English throughout:** "optimise", "colour", "prioritise", "behaviour", "analyse". Never American spellings.
- **Avoid corporate filler:** "leverage", "synergy", "holistic", "best-in-class", "end-to-end", "robust solution". If a phrase could appear in any SaaS product's copy, find a more specific alternative.

### 4.4 Numbers and Data
- Use specific numbers over vague claims: "under two minutes" not "quickly", "six dimensions" not "multiple areas".
- Percentages and stats add authority. Use them when available.
- Score ranges should always be contextualised: "70-89: Good — you're doing well, but there are issues worth addressing."

## 5. Headline & CTA Patterns

### Headlines
Headlines are editorial and confident. They make a clear promise or provoke curiosity.

**Patterns that work:**
- Declarative: "The clarity your website deserves."
- Question-based: "Ready to improve your web presence?"
- Outcome-focused: "Making the web work for everyone."
- Numbered/specific: "Four pillars of website health."

**Patterns to avoid:**
- Hyperbolic: "The ULTIMATE website tool!!!"
- Vague: "Better websites, better business."
- Clickbait: "You won't believe what's wrong with your website."

### CTAs
CTAs should feel like invitations, not demands. They are direct but never pushy.

**Primary CTAs:**
- "Start Free Audit"
- "Run Your First Audit"
- "Get Started — It's Free"

**Secondary CTAs:**
- "Learn More"
- "See How It Works"
- "View Pricing"

**Blog CTAs:**
- "If you'd like to see how your site stacks up, run a free audit."
- "Try it for yourself — no credit card required."

**Avoid:**
- "Buy Now", "Sign Up Today", "Don't Miss Out", "Act Fast"
- Countdown timers, fake urgency, FOMO language

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- Lead with value, not features — explain *why it matters* before *what it does*
- Explain technical concepts in plain English immediately after using them
- Use specific numbers, tools, and examples over vague claims
- Acknowledge trade-offs honestly ("For many smaller sites, that might be all you need")
- Use British English spelling throughout
- Use em dashes for asides and clarity
- Keep CTAs warm and inviting
- Write as though you're speaking to a smart business owner who isn't technical
- Use "we" for Kritano and "I" for founder's blog voice
- Include practical, actionable guidance with every finding or recommendation

### DON'T:
- Use jargon without explanation
- Be purely promotional — always lead with education or value
- Create artificial urgency or use fear-based selling
- Use passive voice when active is more natural
- Use American English spelling
- Use exclamation marks in website or product copy
- Talk about competitors by name (our CLAUDE.md prohibits this)
- Make claims without evidence or reasoning
- Use generic SaaS buzzwords ("leverage", "synergy", "robust", "seamless")
- Write differently for different audiences without good reason — consistency is key

## 7. Tone Calibration

### Formality Spectrum

```
Casual --------[----X-----]--------- Formal
              Blog    Website    UI
```

- **Blog posts:** 35% formal — conversational, uses contractions, personal voice
- **Website copy:** 50% formal — editorial, confident, still accessible
- **Product UI:** 60% formal — precise, supportive, scannable
- **Email (transactional):** 55% formal — clear, warm, action-oriented
- **Email (marketing):** 40% formal — engaging, conversational, value-led
- **Documentation:** 65% formal — thorough, structured, plain language

### Warmth Level: Medium-High
Professional friendliness, not corporate friendliness. The reader should feel guided, not sold to.

### Urgency Level: Low
Our approach is "here's what's wrong, here's how to fix it, start when you're ready." The only exception is genuine scarcity (e.g. early access spots remaining) — and even then, we state the fact plainly without manufactured pressure.

## 8. Content Pillars

Kritano content should reflect the product's six audit dimensions, not just one. The four primary content themes are:

1. **Accessibility** — WCAG compliance, inclusive design, legal requirements
2. **SEO** — Technical SEO, rankings, structured data, search visibility
3. **Security** — Headers, SSL, vulnerabilities, data protection
4. **Performance** — Core Web Vitals, speed, optimisation

Plus two supporting themes:
5. **Content Quality** — Readability, E-E-A-T, content structure
6. **AI Readiness** — AEO, answer engines, AI-citable content

Blog content should be **balanced across all six pillars**, not weighted toward any single one. When planning content, ensure each pillar gets regular coverage.

## 9. Sample Voice Comparison

### Too Casual (chrisgarlick.com voice, unfiltered):
> "Have you ever been on a website waiting to read something important and it just takes so long to load that you instantly leave? I think it's safe to say we've all been there."

### Too Corporate (generic SaaS):
> "Our comprehensive website performance analysis solution leverages industry-leading scanning technology to deliver actionable insights that drive measurable improvements."

### Kritano Voice (the target):
> "Most website owners don't realise their site takes six seconds to load on mobile — long enough for half their visitors to leave. Kritano measures your Core Web Vitals and shows you exactly what's slowing things down, from uncompressed images to render-blocking scripts."

**What makes it work:** Specific (six seconds, Core Web Vitals, render-blocking scripts), empathetic (acknowledges the owner doesn't know), constructive (tells you what you'll learn), and direct (no wasted words).

## 10. Key Phrases & Language

### Brand-Specific Terms
| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| "Website health" | Our primary metaphor — sites have vital signs |
| "Audit" / "Health check" | What we do — never "scan" alone (too clinical) |
| "Findings" / "Issues" | What we surface — never "errors" or "problems" (too negative) |
| "Scores" | Our measurement system — always contextualised with guidance |
| "Pillars" | The six audit dimensions |
| "Guidance" / "Recommendations" | What we provide alongside findings |

### Phrases That Sound Like Us
- "The clarity your website deserves"
- "No jargon, no confusion"
- "Scores, issues, and practical guidance"
- "We'd rather show you 10 real issues than 100 false positives"
- "Start when you're ready"
- "No PhD required"
- "Complete visibility in minutes, not weeks"

### Phrases That Don't Sound Like Us
- "Unlock your website's full potential"
- "Best-in-class scanning engine"
- "Seamless end-to-end solution"
- "Supercharge your SEO"
- "Don't let your competitors get ahead"

## 11. Relationship to Founder Voice

The `docs/marketing/brand_voice.md` file contains a detailed analysis of Chris Garlick's personal writing style from chrisgarlick.com. That document remains a useful **founder voice reference** for understanding the human personality behind the brand.

**How the two relate:**

| Attribute | Founder Voice (chrisgarlick.com) | Kritano Voice |
|---|---|---|
| **Archetype** | The Friendly Expert | The Sage |
| **Formality** | Lower — chatty, colloquial | Higher — editorial, precise |
| **Person** | First singular ("I") | First plural ("we") on site, singular in blog |
| **Technical depth** | Explains basics | Explains at a deeper level, still accessibly |
| **CTAs** | "Get in touch" | "Start Free Audit" |
| **Colloquialisms** | Frequent ("arm and a leg", "bailed") | Occasional, only when natural |
| **Opinions** | "In my honest opinion..." | Stated directly, backed by data |

The founder's voice flows through the blog (especially opinion pieces and the launch post). The Kritano brand voice governs everything else. When in doubt, ask: "Would a trusted consultant say it this way?" If yes, it's on-brand.
