# Off-Page & Link Building Strategy - Kritano

## Linkable Assets (Content That Earns Links)

### 1. Original Research - "State of Website Health" Report
- **Concept:** Scan 1,000+ websites and publish aggregate findings across all 6 pillars
- **Data points:** Average scores per pillar, most common issues, industry benchmarks, accessibility pass rates, security header adoption
- **Why it earns links:** Original data is the #1 link magnet. Journalists, bloggers, and researchers cite data studies heavily.
- **Format:** Blog post + infographic + shareable social cards
- **Target:** Annual report, published Q1 each year

### 2. Free Tools
- **Free website audit** (already exists - the free tier)
- **WCAG quick checker** - a lightweight, instant single-page accessibility scan
- **Security headers checker** - enter a URL, get a security headers grade
- **Content quality score** - instant E-E-A-T + readability score for any URL
- **Why they earn links:** Free tools are the most linked-to content type in SaaS. Every "best tools" roundup includes them.

### 3. Comprehensive Guides (Pillar Pages)
- The 5 pillar pages are themselves linkable assets
- Long-form, regularly updated, authoritative reference content
- Target: become the go-to reference for each topic

### 4. Benchmark Data
- "Website Performance Benchmarks by Industry" (already exists as blog post)
- Expand with more industries and annual updates
- Shareable stats that other writers will cite

## Link Building Priorities (Ordered)

### Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)
1. **List on comparison sites:** G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, AlternativeTo
2. **Submit free tool to "best tools" roundups:** Pitch editors of existing roundup articles
3. **Social presence building:** Reddit comments, LinkedIn posts, X threads (already happening via /trend)
4. **Directory listings:** Web accessibility tool directories, SaaS directories

### Phase 2: Content-Driven (Month 2-4)
5. **Publish "State of Website Health" data study** - pitch to journalists
6. **Guest post on 3-5 industry blogs** (Smashing, CSS-Tricks, SEJ)
7. **HARO responses** - 2-3 per week on relevant queries
8. **Broken link building** - find broken links to dead audit tools, suggest Kritano

### Phase 3: Authority (Month 4-6)
9. **Digital PR campaign** around annual data release
10. **Expert commentary** in industry publications
11. **Unlinked brand mention conversion** - find mentions of Kritano without links, request links
12. **Partnership content** with complementary tools (hosting providers, CMS platforms)

## What to Avoid

- Buying links from link farms or PBNs
- Large-scale reciprocal link exchanges
- Exact-match anchor text manipulation
- Low-quality guest posts on irrelevant sites
- Automated link building tools or services
- Comment spam or forum spam
- Paid advertorial links without nofollow

## AI Visibility (Off-Page)

AI engines build their citation preferences from the broader web. Off-page actions that increase AI citation likelihood:

1. **Get cited on authoritative sites** - guest posts on high-DA blogs
2. **Appear in comparison/roundup content** - "best website audit tools" articles
3. **Build Reddit presence** - AI engines heavily weight Reddit discussions
4. **Consistent brand mentions** - the more Kritano appears in context of "website audit", the stronger the entity signal
5. **Product Hunt launch** - creates a strong, crawlable entity page that AI engines reference
