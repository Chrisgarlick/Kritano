# AI & Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) - Kritano

## Why This Matters for Kritano

Kritano already audits AEO readiness for its clients. The product itself must be visible in AI-generated answers when users ask questions like:
- "What's the best website audit tool?"
- "How do I check if my website is accessible?"
- "What tools check content quality for SEO?"

AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Bing Copilot) cite 2-7 sources per answer. Getting into those citations is the new "page one".

---

## Content Structure for AI Extraction

### The Concise Answer Pattern

Every major section on Kritano's blog must follow this pattern:

```
## [Question as heading]

[1-2 sentence direct answer that AI can extract verbatim.]

[Expanded explanation with supporting detail, examples, data.]
```

**Example:**
```
## What is a website audit?

A website audit is a comprehensive check of your site's health across SEO,
accessibility, security, performance, and content quality. It identifies
issues that affect your search rankings, user experience, and compliance.

Here's what a thorough audit covers...
```

The first 1-2 sentences are what AI engines extract. They must be:
- Self-contained (make sense without the heading)
- Factual and specific (not vague or promotional)
- Under 50 words (AI engines truncate)

### FAQ Sections (Every Key Page)

Every blog post and landing page must include a FAQ section with:
- 3-5 genuine questions users ask about the topic
- Concise, direct answers (2-4 sentences each)
- Wrapped in FAQPage schema markup
- Questions phrased as natural language ("How do I..." not "Steps to...")

### Named Authors with Credentials

AI engines prioritise attributed content:
- Every blog post: "Written by Chris Garlick" with a visible bio
- Bio should include: role, experience, relevant qualifications
- Consistent author entity across all content (same name, same bio structure)

## Schema for AI Visibility

| Page Type | Schema | AI Benefit |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Blog posts | Article + FAQPage | Headlines, dates, and FAQs appear in AI answers |
| How-to content | HowTo | Step-by-step instructions extracted by AI |
| Pricing page | Product + Offer + FAQPage | Pricing info cited in "best tools" answers |
| Homepage | WebApplication + Organization | Brand entity recognition |
| Comparison posts | Table markup + FAQPage | Feature comparisons extracted verbatim |

## Topical Depth Strategy

AI engines strongly prefer sites with deep, interconnected coverage of a topic over sites with isolated articles.

**Kritano's advantage:** The 5 topic clusters (Website Auditing, Accessibility, Performance, Security, Content Quality) create exactly the kind of topical depth AI engines reward. When a user asks "what's the best website audit tool?", an AI engine is more likely to cite a site that has 20+ articles about auditing than one with a single product page.

**Priority actions:**
1. Build out all 5 pillar pages (comprehensive guides)
2. Ensure every cluster page links back to its pillar
3. Cross-link between clusters where natural
4. Publish consistently (2-3 posts per month minimum)
5. Update existing content regularly (AI engines prefer fresh content)

## Brand Entity Signals

For AI engines to cite Kritano, they need to recognise it as an entity:

**Short-term (1-3 months):**
- Consistent brand mentions on social media (X, LinkedIn, Reddit)
- Guest posts on industry blogs mentioning Kritano
- Product Hunt launch (creates a strong entity signal)
- Listings on comparison sites (G2, Capterra, Product Hunt)

**Medium-term (3-6 months):**
- Being referenced in "best tools" roundup articles
- Getting cited in industry reports or studies
- Building presence on platforms AI engines crawl heavily (Reddit, Stack Overflow, Quora)
- Earned media coverage in industry publications

**Long-term (6-12 months):**
- Wikipedia mention (requires notability)
- Knowledge panel in Google (requires entity recognition)
- Consistent citation across multiple AI engines
