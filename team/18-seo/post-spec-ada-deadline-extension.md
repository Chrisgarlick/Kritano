## 5. FAQ Questions (for FAQPage Schema)

These are optimised for AI extraction and "People Also Ask" boxes:

1. **What is the new ADA web accessibility deadline?**
   Answer: The DOJ extended the compliance deadline for large public entities (population 50,000+) from April 24, 2026 to April 26, 2027. Smaller entities now have until April 26, 2028.

2. **Does the ADA web accessibility deadline apply to private businesses?**
   Answer: The DOJ's Title II rule applies specifically to state and local government websites. However, private businesses face separate ADA obligations under Title III, and the number of accessibility lawsuits against private websites continues to rise.

3. **What standard do websites need to meet for ADA compliance?**
   Answer: The DOJ requires websites to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA - the international standard for web accessibility. This covers alt text for images, colour contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, form labels, and more.

4. **How do I check if my website is ADA compliant?**
   Answer: Run a website accessibility audit using a tool that checks against WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines. This will identify specific violations like missing alt text, poor contrast, and keyboard traps, with guidance on how to fix each one.

5. **Why was the ADA accessibility deadline extended?**
   Answer: The DOJ stated it "overestimated the capabilities" of covered entities to comply in time, citing limited financial, technical, and staffing resources. Disability advocates have criticised the delay, calling it "unconscionable."

## 7. AI Extraction Structure

**The Concise Answer Pattern - apply to every H2:**

Each section should open with a 1-2 sentence direct answer that AI engines can extract verbatim, followed by expanded context.

**Example for the lead section:**

```
## What Just Happened?

The US Department of Justice has extended the ADA web accessibility
compliance deadline by one year - from April 24, 2026 to April 26, 2027
for large public entities, and from April 26, 2027 to April 26, 2028
for smaller entities.

[Expanded context follows...]
```

**The first 50 words of every section must be self-contained and factual.** AI engines extract these as citations. Don't start sections with rhetorical questions or vague set-up sentences.

**FAQ answers must be under 50 words each.** AI engines truncate beyond this. Front-load the answer, add nuance after.

**Named author with bio:** Include "Written by Chris Garlick" with the standard author bio component. E-E-A-T signal for both Google and AI engines.
