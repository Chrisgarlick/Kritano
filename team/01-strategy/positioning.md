# Positioning — Kritano

## Positioning Statement

> For **agencies, freelancers, and growing businesses** who need to understand why their website isn't performing — and how to fix it — **Kritano** is a **website intelligence platform** that audits SEO, content quality, accessibility, security, and performance in a single scan, then prioritises exactly what to fix first. Unlike Lighthouse (surface-level), Semrush (SEO-only depth), and Siteimprove (enterprise pricing), Kritano combines **content intelligence** (E-E-A-T, AEO, readability, engagement scoring) with **actionable remediation** at a price point that starts free and scales with you.

## Category

**Website Intelligence Platform** — not "website auditor", not "accessibility checker", not "SEO tool."

The category matters because it defines who Kritano competes with in the buyer's mind. "Auditor" invites comparison with free tools. "Intelligence platform" invites comparison with expensive enterprise tools — and then surprises with the price.

## Core Positioning Pillars

### Pillar 1: Content Intelligence (Unique)
Kritano is the only tool that scores content the way Google's algorithms evaluate it. E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust), AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation), readability, engagement markers, and content structure — unified into a Content Quality Score. No competitor offers this combination.

**Proof point:** 7 content analysis sub-modules running 400+ checks across quality, readability, structure, engagement, E-E-A-T, AEO, and keyword analysis.

### Pillar 2: Depth Across Six Categories (Competitive Parity+)
SEO, Accessibility, Security, Performance, Content, and Structured Data — all in one scan. Competitors force you to use 2-3 tools to get the same coverage.

**Proof point:** 4,600+ lines of check logic across 7 engine files and 7 content sub-modules. 500+ individual rules.

### Pillar 3: Actionable Output (Differentiator)
Every finding answers three questions: what's wrong, why it matters, and how to fix it. Shareable reports, accessibility statements, and fix snippets turn diagnosis into action. The audit is the beginning, not the end.

**Proof point:** Shareable public reports (48h links), accessibility statement generator, template-based fix snippets (coming), priority ranking by business impact (coming).

### Pillar 4: Agency-Grade at Indie Pricing (Value)
White-label exports, team workspaces, per-site collaboration, portfolio management — features that enterprise tools charge thousands for, starting at $99/month for agencies.

**Proof point:** 5-tier structure from $0 to custom enterprise. Full white-label PDF branding at Agency tier. Unlimited seats. 50 sites per account.

## Who Kritano Is NOT For

- **Enterprise IT teams** running compliance audits across 500+ internal properties (they need Siteimprove or Deque)
- **Pure SEO specialists** who need rank tracking, backlink analysis, and competitor keyword research (they need Semrush or Ahrefs)
- **Developers** who just want a quick Lighthouse score (they'll use Lighthouse directly)

Being clear about who we're *not* for sharpens the message for who we *are* for.

## Personas

### Persona 1: "Agency Alex" — Digital Agency Owner/Director

- **Role:** Owner or digital director at a 5-30 person agency
- **Company:** Web design/development agency serving SMB clients
- **Problem:** Managing website quality across 15-40 client sites is manual, inconsistent, and time-consuming. Current approach: run Lighthouse per-site, compile results in spreadsheets, send PDFs to clients. No unified view. Can't prove the value of ongoing retainers.
- **Current tools:** Lighthouse (free, manual), Screaming Frog (SEO crawling), maybe Semrush (expensive, SEO-focused)
- **Pain:** Spends 4+ hours per client per month on manual auditing. Can't show improvement trends. White-labelling reports requires custom templates. No way to prioritise which client site needs attention most.
- **Success looks like:** Single dashboard showing all client sites ranked by urgency. Branded reports generated in one click. Scheduled audits that catch regressions before clients notice. Prove retainer value with trend data.
- **Key objection:** "Can I white-label this? My clients can't see 'Kritano' branding."
- **Channels:** LinkedIn, agency Slack communities, WordPress meetups, Clutch, DesignRush

### Persona 2: "Founder Fran" — Solo Founder / Small Business Owner

- **Role:** Non-technical founder or marketing lead at a 1-10 person company
- **Company:** SaaS startup, e-commerce, professional services
- **Problem:** Knows their website "should be better" but doesn't know where to start. Has heard of SEO and accessibility but doesn't understand the technical details. Can't afford to hire a specialist for every issue.
- **Current tools:** Google Search Console (confusing), maybe PageSpeed Insights (surface-level), nothing for accessibility or content quality
- **Pain:** Feels overwhelmed by technical jargon. Doesn't know what's important vs what's noise. Wants someone to say "fix this first, here's how."
- **Success looks like:** A clear score that says "your website is at X out of 100." A prioritised list of fixes in plain English. Knowing their site meets accessibility requirements without becoming an expert.
- **Key objection:** "I'm not technical. Will I understand the results?"
- **Channels:** Indie Hackers, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, founder newsletters, Google search ("how to improve website SEO")

### Persona 3: "Developer Dana" — Frontend/Fullstack Developer

- **Role:** Senior developer or tech lead responsible for site quality
- **Company:** In-house at a mid-size company, or freelance
- **Problem:** Wants to catch accessibility and SEO regressions before they ship. Current approach: manual Lighthouse runs, sporadic axe-core checks, no CI integration. Quality is reactive, not proactive.
- **Current tools:** Lighthouse (CLI), axe-core (browser extension), ESLint plugins, maybe Calibre or SpeedCurve
- **Pain:** No automated quality gate in CI/CD. Accessibility issues found in production, not development. No way to track improvements over time across deploys.
- **Success looks like:** A CLI command or GitHub Action that runs on every PR. Score drops block merge. Dashboard shows trends per deploy. API for custom integrations.
- **Key objection:** "Does it have an API? Can I run it in CI?"
- **Channels:** GitHub, Dev.to, Hacker News, JavaScript/React communities, Stack Overflow
