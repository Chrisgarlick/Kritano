# Competitive Analysis — Kritano

## Direct Competitors

### Google Lighthouse / PageSpeed Insights
- **What they do well:** Free, instant, trusted by developers, integrated into Chrome DevTools. The industry standard for quick performance and accessibility checks.
- **Where they fall short:** Surface-level checks only. No content analysis. No E-E-A-T or AEO. No scheduling, no trends, no team features, no exports beyond screenshots. Single-page only — no site-wide crawling.
- **Kritano advantage:** Site-wide crawling (up to 10,000 pages), content intelligence, scheduling, trend tracking, team features, white-label exports. Deeper check coverage with 500+ rules vs Lighthouse's ~100.
- **Their advantage over us:** Free, ubiquitous, zero onboarding friction. Developers already use it.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM — they're the free baseline everyone compares against, not a paid competitor.

### Screaming Frog SEO Spider
- **What they do well:** Deep technical SEO crawling. Excellent for large-site audits. Desktop app, one-time license (£199/yr). The standard for SEO agencies.
- **Where they fall short:** SEO only — no accessibility, no security, no content quality, no performance. Desktop-only, no web dashboard. No scheduling. No team features. Exports are raw data, not reports.
- **Kritano advantage:** Six audit categories vs one. Web-based with scheduling and team features. Content intelligence. Accessibility compliance reporting. White-label PDFs.
- **Their advantage over us:** Deeper technical SEO crawling for very large sites (100k+ pages). Established in agency workflows for a decade. One-time payment vs subscription.
- **Threat level:** LOW-MEDIUM — different category (desktop tool vs web platform), some audience overlap.

### Semrush / Ahrefs / Moz
- **What they do well:** Comprehensive SEO suites — rank tracking, backlink analysis, keyword research, competitor analysis, content gap analysis. Massive datasets. Established market leaders.
- **Where they fall short:** SEO-focused. Accessibility is absent or minimal. No security checks. Content analysis is keyword-centric, not quality-centric. Pricing starts at $130-200/mo. Overwhelming for non-SEO-specialists.
- **Kritano advantage:** Broader audit scope (a11y, security, content quality, performance). Content intelligence (E-E-A-T, AEO) that Semrush doesn't offer. Dramatically lower pricing. Simpler UX for non-specialists.
- **Their advantage over us:** Vastly deeper SEO features (rank tracking, backlinks, keyword research). Established brand. Huge datasets. Kritano doesn't do keyword research or rank tracking — nor should it.
- **Threat level:** LOW — different primary value proposition. Complementary rather than competitive.

### Siteimprove
- **What they do well:** Enterprise accessibility compliance. EAA/WCAG reporting. Large-scale site monitoring. AI-powered fix suggestions (recently added). Established with enterprise procurement.
- **Where they fall short:** Enterprise pricing ($500-2,000+/mo). No free tier. Slow onboarding. No content intelligence. Accessibility-first, light on SEO depth.
- **Kritano advantage:** Content intelligence. Broader audit scope. Price (dramatically cheaper). Self-serve signup vs sales calls. Cold prospect pipeline as GTM.
- **Their advantage over us:** Enterprise compliance credentials. Dedicated customer success. AI fix suggestions already shipping. Established EAA reporting.
- **Threat level:** HIGH for enterprise segment, LOW for indie/agency segment.

### Lumar (formerly DeepCrawl)
- **What they do well:** Technical SEO at scale. Site architecture analysis. Recently added accessibility monitoring. API-first. CI/CD integration.
- **Where they fall short:** Enterprise pricing. Complex interface. No content quality analysis. Limited accessibility depth vs dedicated a11y tools.
- **Kritano advantage:** Content intelligence. Broader check coverage. Self-serve. Price. Content quality scoring they don't have.
- **Their advantage over us:** Enterprise scale. CI/CD integration already built. Deeper crawl architecture analysis.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM — they're moving into Kritano's space from the enterprise end.

## Indirect Competitors

### axe-core / Deque (Accessibility-Only)
- **Position:** The gold standard for WCAG testing. Used by developers as a library and browser extension.
- **Overlap:** Accessibility checks only. Kritano uses axe-core as part of its own accessibility engine.
- **Differentiation:** Kritano wraps axe-core in 5 additional audit categories and a business-user-friendly interface.

### GTmetrix / Pingdom / WebPageTest (Performance-Only)
- **Position:** Dedicated performance testing tools.
- **Overlap:** Performance scoring. Kritano includes performance as one of six categories.
- **Differentiation:** Performance is a commodity. Kritano's value is the *combination* of performance with SEO, content, accessibility, and security.

### Clearscope / SurferSEO / MarketMuse (Content Optimisation)
- **Position:** AI-powered content optimisation for SEO.
- **Overlap:** Content quality assessment. These tools help you write *better* content; Kritano *audits* existing content.
- **Differentiation:** Kritano's content analysis is diagnostic (what's wrong with what you have), not prescriptive (what to write). Different use case, but the E-E-A-T and AEO scoring could evolve into prescriptive territory.

## Strategic Implications

1. **Don't compete on SEO depth with Semrush/Ahrefs.** We'll lose. Compete on breadth (6 categories) and content intelligence (unique).
2. **Don't compete on enterprise compliance with Siteimprove.** We'll lose on credentials. Compete on price, self-serve, and content intelligence.
3. **Do compete on "replacing 3 tools with 1."** The indie/agency buyer currently uses Lighthouse + Screaming Frog + manual checks. Kritano replaces all three.
4. **The Content Quality Score is our moat.** No competitor has E-E-A-T + AEO + readability + engagement scoring. Protect this advantage by continuing to invest in content analysis depth.
5. **Price is a weapon.** Free → $19 → $49 → $99 undercuts every paid competitor by 50-80%. Don't raise prices prematurely.
