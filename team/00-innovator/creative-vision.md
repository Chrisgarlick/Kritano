# Creative Vision — Kritano

## The Big Idea

Kritano isn't a scanner. It's your website's doctor — it diagnoses what's wrong, explains why it matters, and writes the prescription. While every other tool in the market hands you a 400-line spreadsheet of issues and says "good luck", Kritano tells you exactly what to fix, in what order, and gives you the code to do it. It's the difference between a blood test result and a doctor who reads it to you.

The platform already has the diagnostic depth — six audit engines, E-E-A-T analysis, AEO scoring, content quality metrics — that puts it ahead of most competitors on *breadth*. What's missing is the last mile: turning diagnosis into action. That last mile is where Kritano becomes indispensable.

## Why It Matters

Website owners — freelancers, agencies, marketing teams — don't want to know they have 47 accessibility issues. They want those issues *gone*. The current market forces them through a painful cycle: scan with one tool, understand the output with another, fix it manually, re-scan to check. Kritano can collapse that into one step.

For agencies managing 20+ client sites, this isn't a convenience — it's a business model shift. An agency that can audit, remediate, and prove compliance in a single platform will close deals that others can't. Kritano becomes infrastructure, not a tool.

## The "Wow" Moments

1. **"Fix This" button** — A finding card shows a heading hierarchy violation. The user clicks "Fix This" and gets a code snippet they can paste directly into their CMS. Not generic advice — actual code, for their actual page, with their actual HTML structure.

2. **Compliance Passport** — A European client asks "are we EAA compliant?" The agency clicks one button and generates a branded compliance report that maps every WCAG finding to the specific EN 301 549 clause it violates, with a traffic-light status and remediation timeline. They hand it to the client's legal team. Deal closed.

3. **The Score Drop Alert** — A scheduled audit runs at 2am. By 6am, the site owner has an email: "Your accessibility score dropped 12 points overnight. 3 new critical issues appeared after yesterday's deploy. Here's what changed." Not just monitoring — *intelligent* monitoring that connects score changes to events.

4. **The Viral Score Card** — After an audit completes, a "Share Your Score" prompt appears with a beautifully designed, branded score card. The user shares it on LinkedIn. Their network sees "Kritano" with their score. It's social proof for the user and organic marketing for Kritano simultaneously.

5. **One-Command CI Gate** — A developer adds `kritano audit --fail-under 80` to their GitHub Action. Every PR now gets an accessibility and SEO check. If the score drops below 80, the PR fails. No dashboard needed — Kritano lives in the developer's existing workflow.

## Unconventional Angles

1. **Flip the business model for agencies.** Most SaaS tools charge agencies per seat. Kritano already charges per *site*. Push this further: let agencies white-label the entire platform and resell it to their clients. Kritano becomes the engine behind every agency's "proprietary" auditing tool. Revenue shifts from per-user to platform licensing.

2. **Make the audit results *public* by default for verified sites.** Create a searchable, browsable directory of website health scores (opt-in, verified owners only). Turn Kritano into the "Trustpilot for website quality." Sites that score well get a verified badge. Sites that score poorly have incentive to improve. The directory becomes Kritano's SEO engine — every indexed score page is a new keyword.

3. **Treat content quality as seriously as accessibility.** Every competitor focuses on technical SEO and WCAG compliance. Kritano already has E-E-A-T, AEO, readability, engagement, and content structure analysis — but it's positioned as secondary to accessibility. Flip this: make content quality a *first-class citizen* in the branding. "The only tool that audits your content like Google does" is a stronger differentiator than "another WCAG scanner."
