# Innovation Brief — Kritano

## Recommended Direction

**Lead with content intelligence, not accessibility compliance.**

Every competitor in the audit space leads with accessibility or SEO. Kritano has something they don't: E-E-A-T scoring, AEO analysis, content quality metrics, readability analysis, and engagement measurement. These are built, shipped, and working — but they're positioned as secondary features.

The recommended direction is to elevate **content quality** to equal billing with SEO and accessibility. The tagline should evolve from "website auditing platform" to something like "the platform that scores your website the way Google does." This positions Kritano in a less crowded space (content intelligence) while retaining the audit breadth that makes it competitive on SEO and accessibility.

The compliance angle (EAA) should be the *second* differentiator — time-sensitive, high-urgency, and largely a presentation exercise on top of existing WCAG data.

AI fix suggestions should be the *third* differentiator — the feature that converts evaluators into paying users. "See the problem, get the fix."

## Key Creative Bets

### 1. Content Quality Score as a First-Class Metric
Unify E-E-A-T, AEO, readability, engagement, and content structure into a single Content Quality Score. Give it its own dashboard, trend tracking, and prominence equal to SEO and Accessibility scores. This is the "only Kritano does this" feature.

### 2. Compliance Passport (EAA)
Map existing WCAG findings to EN 301 549 clauses. Add a compliance status indicator and generate compliance reports. 80% presentation, 20% engineering. The European Accessibility Act is in force now — every month without this feature is a month of missed positioning.

### 3. Fix Snippets (Template-Based First, AI Later)
Don't wait for full AI integration. Pre-write fix templates for the 50 most common findings. Missing alt text, missing lang attribute, broken heading hierarchy, missing meta descriptions — these have predictable, copy-paste solutions. Ship the "Fix This" button *now* with templates, then upgrade to AI-generated fixes later. Users get value immediately; the AI upgrade becomes a feature enhancement, not a prerequisite.

### 4. Developer Workflow Integration
Complete the webhook UI (infrastructure exists). Build the GitHub Action. Add the CLI. These are the features that make Kritano sticky for technical teams — once it's in the CI/CD pipeline, switching costs become real.

### 5. Agency Portfolio Dashboard
The feature that closes Agency tier deals. A single screen showing all client sites, ranked by urgency, with bulk actions. The data and queries already exist — this is primarily a frontend build.

## Risks of Playing It Safe

If Kritano launches to founding members as "a comprehensive website scanner":
- It competes head-to-head with Lighthouse (free), Screaming Frog (cheap), and Semrush (dominant)
- It has no answer to "why should I switch from my current tool?"
- The content analysis depth goes unnoticed because it's buried under generic audit results
- The agency positioning lacks the portfolio tools that agencies actually need
- The compliance story has no dedicated surface — it's just "we check WCAG"

The risk isn't that Kritano is bad — it's that it's *invisible* in a crowded market. The features that make it unique (content intelligence, E-E-A-T, AEO) need to be the features that are loudest.

## Creative Constraints to Embrace

### Less Is More on the Dashboard
The dashboard currently shows recent audits and stats. For new users, it should show *one thing*: their content quality score with a single "here's how to improve it" recommendation. Progressive complexity: start simple, reveal depth as users engage.

### Opinionated Defaults
Don't make users choose which checks to run. Default to "everything your tier allows" and surface the results in order of business impact, not alphabetical order. The audit should feel like a conversation, not a spreadsheet.

### Time Pressure as a Feature
The EAA is in force. Use this. Show countdown timers, compliance status, and urgency indicators. "Your site has 14 EAA compliance issues. Enforcement began June 2025." Urgency drives upgrades.

### The Fix Is the Product
Every finding card should answer: "What is this? Why does it matter? How do I fix it?" If any finding can't answer all three, it's incomplete. The fix is what users pay for — the diagnosis is just the hook.
