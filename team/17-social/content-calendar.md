**LinkedIn -- Data Insight**
> Security headers are the seatbelts of the web.
>
> Most websites don't have them.
>
> I checked 50 UK agency websites this month. Here's what's missing:

> - 78% have no Content Security Policy (CSP)

### Tuesday 22 April

**LinkedIn -- Industry Commentary**
> The European Accessibility Act is here. Most businesses aren't ready.
>
> Since June 2025, digital products and services in the EU must meet accessibility requirements. That includes websites, mobile apps, and e-commerce platforms.
>
> But here's what most people miss:
>
> The EAA doesn't just apply to EU-based companies. If you sell to EU customers, you're in scope.
>
> That means UK agencies building sites for European clients need to care about this. UK e-commerce shops shipping to the EU need to care about this.
>
> Three things to do now:
>
> 1. Audit your website against WCAG 2.1 AA (the baseline the EAA references)
> 2. Fix critical issues -- missing alt text, poor contrast, keyboard traps
> 3. Create an accessibility statement (Kritano generates these free)
>
> This isn't just compliance. It's about making the web work for everyone.
>
> Is your agency preparing clients for EAA compliance?
>

**LinkedIn -- Quick Tip**
> 3 things you can check on your website right now (takes 5 minutes):

### Wednesday 30 April

**LinkedIn -- Quick Tip (Structured Data)**
> Want your website to stand out in Google search results?
>
> Add structured data.
>
> It's the code that tells search engines exactly what your page is about -- and it can unlock rich snippets (stars, FAQs, prices, events) in search results.
>
> Three types most websites should have:
>
> 1. Organization schema -- tells Google who you are
> 2. FAQ schema -- your FAQ answers appear directly in search
> 3. Article schema -- for blog posts (publish date, author, etc.)
>
> You can test yours at schema.org/validator
>
> Does your website have structured data?
>
> #TechnicalSEO #StructuredData #SEO
