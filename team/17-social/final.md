# Social Media Plan -- Kritano (Final Consolidated Document)

> Updated April 2026. Practical, day-by-day execution plan for a solo founder at week 2.

---

## Executive Summary

Two weeks of slow growth is normal. The algorithm needs time to learn who you are, and your audience needs time to discover you. The plan: spend 80% of social time engaging with others' content, 20% creating your own. Focus on LinkedIn (primary B2B channel) and X (dev/accessibility community), with Reddit and Instagram as supporting channels.

**Total weekly time: ~8 hours** (realistic for a solo founder also building product)

**X: Daily**
Mon: Audit insight | Tue: Blog share | Wed: Thread | Thu: Blog share | Fri: Build in public | Sat: Quote-tweet | Sun: Hot take

**Instagram: Daily (batch Sundays)**
3x carousel, 2x stat graphic, 1x reel, daily stories.

## Daily Engagement Checklist

This is the most important part of the entire plan:

- [ ] LinkedIn: Comment on 5-10 posts from target audience (15 min)
- [ ] LinkedIn: Reply to all comments on your posts (5 min)
- [ ] X: Search #a11y #webdev #WCAG, reply to 3-5 posts (10 min)
- [ ] X: Reply to all comments (5 min)
- [ ] Instagram: Reply + engage with 5 accounts (10 min)
- [ ] Reddit (Mon/Wed/Fri): Browse and write 2 helpful comments (15 min)

**Total: 30-45 min/day. More impactful than any individual post.**

## Growth Tactics

1. **Value Comment Strategy:** Comment on posts from target audience with 3-5 sentences of genuine insight. This puts you in front of THEIR audience. 10x/day on LinkedIn.

2. **Data Post Formula:** Posts with real numbers outperform everything on LinkedIn. "I audited 50 sites -- here's what I found."

3. **Build in Public Threads:** Weekly on X with real numbers. Users, revenue, lessons, failures.

4. **Reddit Expert Answers:** Comprehensive, genuinely helpful answers in target subreddits. One great answer can drive traffic for months.

5. **Free Audit Offers:** Offer to audit people's sites in LinkedIn comments, Reddit threads, X replies. Creates goodwill, content, and social proof.

## What NOT to Do

1. Don't buy followers or use engagement pods
2. Don't post and ghost -- always engage with replies
3. Don't be salesy -- lead with value, always
4. Don't compare yourself to established accounts
5. Don't spread too thin -- LinkedIn + X first, add platforms when these work
6. Don't spend more than 30 min on any single post
7. Don't ignore DMs
