# Reddit Engagement Playbook

## Core Philosophy

On Reddit, you are not a brand. You are a person who happens to have built something useful. Every interaction should feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend, not a company's social media manager.

---

## Response Templates

### When someone compliments the product

> Thanks — really appreciate that. If you run into anything weird or have ideas for what we should add next, I'm all ears. Building this thing based on real feedback, not guesswork.

### When someone reports a bug or issue

> Appreciate you flagging this — that's a real bug and I'll fix it. Can you DM me the URL you were scanning? I want to reproduce it on my end. I'll update you when it's sorted.

### When someone criticises the product

> Fair point — [acknowledge the specific criticism]. That's something I'm actively working on. [If you have context, share it: "The reason it works that way currently is X, but I agree it should be Y."] Appreciate the honest feedback.

### When someone asks "how is this different from Lighthouse?"

> Lighthouse is excellent for quick performance and accessibility checks — I use it myself. The difference:
>
> - Kritano scans across 6 categories in one pass (SEO, accessibility, security, performance, content quality, structured data)
> - Content quality scoring (E-E-A-T, readability, engagement) — Lighthouse doesn't touch this
> - Prioritised findings ranked by business impact, not just severity
> - Shareable reports and PDF exports
> - Historical trends across scans
>
> If you just need a quick performance check, Lighthouse is great. If you want the full picture, that's where we come in.

### When someone asks about pricing

> There's a free tier — you can run [X] scans per month at no cost. If you need more scans, scheduled monitoring, or PDF reports, paid plans start at [price]. No credit card required for free.
>
> Happy to answer specific questions about what's included at each tier.

### When someone asks "is this open source?"

> Not currently. The audit engine has taken [X] months to build with 500+ individual checks, so I'm keeping the core proprietary for now. That said, I'm always open to feedback on the methodology and I publish the scoring approach in our docs.

## Response Time Targets

| Context | Target | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Reply to comments on your own post | Within 2 hours | Reddit's algorithm boosts active posts. First 2 hours are critical. |
| Reply to DMs | Within 24 hours | Shows respect and builds relationships. |
| Reply to threads you've been tagged in | Within 12 hours | Being responsive builds reputation. |
| Engaging in other threads | Daily, 15 min | Consistency matters more than speed. |

---

## Escalation Rules

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Someone reports a security vulnerability | Thank them publicly, ask them to DM details, fix immediately. |
| Someone posts misleading claims about accessibility tools in general | Respond with facts and citations. Don't get personal. |
| A thread turns hostile | Disengage. One calm response, then stop. Reddit fights never end well. |
| Someone asks a question you can't answer well | Be honest: "I'm not sure about that one — [tag someone who might know] or check [resource]." |
| Media or journalist reaches out | Respond professionally. Be helpful but don't speculate or overpromise. |

## Community Management

### Building Genuine Presence

1. **Comment-to-post ratio:** Maintain at least 5:1 (5 helpful comments for every self-promotional post). Reddit users check your post history.
2. **Upvote generously.** Upvote good content even from competitors. This isn't a zero-sum game.
3. **Ask questions too.** Don't only answer. Asking genuine questions shows you're a community member, not a marketer.
4. **Share other people's tools.** When someone asks for recommendations and Kritano isn't the right fit, recommend what is. This builds massive trust.
5. **Never delete downvoted posts.** Unless they contain a genuine mistake. Taking your lumps is part of Reddit.

### Handling Self-Promotion Accusations

It will happen. Someone will say "this is just an ad." Response:

> Fair enough — I'm transparent that I built Kritano. I try to make sure every comment I write is helpful on its own, whether or not anyone clicks through. If this one missed the mark, I'll take that feedback. What specifically would be more helpful?

Then move on. Don't over-explain.

## URL Audit Offers

One of the highest-converting Reddit activities is offering free site reviews. Here's the process:

1. **In relevant threads**, offer: "Drop your URL and I'll take a look"
2. **Run the URL through Kritano** to get the data
3. **Write a personalised 4-5 line response** highlighting the top 2-3 issues and 1 positive
4. **Don't just link to the report** — write the insights in your own words
5. **If they want the full report**, offer to DM them the link

This is high-effort but generates:
- Genuine gratitude and upvotes
- "What tool are you using?" questions from others in the thread
- DM conversations that convert to signups
- Reddit karma and reputation

Budget 30 minutes per URL review. Limit to 3-4 per week to stay sustainable.
