# Editorial Filter — What's Worth Covering

Not every trending topic deserves a Kritano post. This filter ensures we only create content that serves the audience, builds authority, and aligns with the brand.

## The Three Gates

Every trend must pass **all three gates** before becoming content:

### Gate 1: Relevance

**Does this directly relate to at least one of the 6 pillars?**

- ✅ Google announces new Core Web Vitals metric → Performance + SEO
- ✅ Major accessibility lawsuit settled → Accessibility
- ✅ New AI search feature changes how sites are cited → AI Readiness
- ❌ General tech industry layoffs → Not relevant
- ❌ Social media algorithm change (unless it affects web traffic) → Not relevant
- ❌ Hardware/device news → Not relevant

**Does it affect website owners, developers, or digital marketers?**

If the answer is "only indirectly" or "only if you squint", skip it.

### Gate 2: Substance

**Is there enough here to say something useful?**

- ✅ Official announcement with details → Strong substance
- ✅ Research study with data → Strong substance
- ✅ Developing story with confirmed facts → Moderate substance
- ⚠️ Rumour or leak without confirmation → Wait for confirmation
- ❌ Vague speculation → Skip
- ❌ Repackaged old news → Skip
- ❌ "X might happen soon" without evidence → Skip

**Can we add insight beyond just reporting?**

Kritano content should always include an expert angle. If all we can do is restate the headline, it's not worth a post.

### Gate 3: Engagement Potential

**Will the audience care?**

Score each trend on these signals:

| Signal | Weight | Examples |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Affects many websites** | High | Google ranking change, WCAG update |
| **Has a strong data point** | High | "73% of websites fail..." |
| **Controversial or debated** | High | AI content policies, overlay lawsuits |
| **Actionable** | High | "Do this now before [deadline]" |
| **Surprising** | Medium | Counter-intuitive findings |
| **Breaking / very recent** | Medium | First 48 hours of a story |
| **Niche but deep** | Low | Browser rendering edge case |
| **Already saturated** | Negative | If every SEO blog already covered it identically |

**Minimum threshold**: At least 2 "High" signals, or 1 "High" + 2 "Medium".

## Angle Selection

Once a trend passes all three gates, choose the Kritano angle:

### Available Angles

| Angle | Tone | Best when... |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **The Explainer** | Educational, clear | Complex topic that needs demystifying |
| **The Hot Take** | Opinionated, confident | We have a strong, defensible opinion |
| **The Data Drop** | Analytical, authoritative | There's a compelling stat to lead with |
| **The Warning** | Urgent, helpful | Something changed and people need to act |
| **The Opportunity** | Optimistic, strategic | A change that early adopters can benefit from |
| **The Myth Buster** | Corrective, honest | Common misunderstanding about the trend |

### Angle Rules

- **Never be alarmist** — even "warning" content should be calm and solution-oriented
- **Always include what to do** — don't just report the problem
- **Credit original sources** — never present others' research as our own
- **Be honest about uncertainty** — if something is developing, say so
- **Avoid bandwagoning** — if every other brand is posting the same take, find a different angle or skip it

## Content Quality Checks

Before finalising any content piece, verify:

- [ ] **Factually accurate** — claims are sourced and verified
- [ ] **Source attributed** — data, quotes, and findings credited to original source
- [ ] **Adds value** — not just restating the headline
- [ ] **Kritano voice** — conversational expert, not corporate robot
- [ ] **British English** — optimise, colour, favour, etc.
- [ ] **No competitor mentions** — never name or recommend competing audit tools
- [ ] **No fear-mongering** — urgent ≠ scary. Be solution-oriented.
- [ ] **Actionable** — reader should know what to do after reading
- [ ] **Timely** — will this still be relevant when it's posted?
- [ ] **Platform-appropriate** — tone and format match the platform

---

## Kill Criteria

**Immediately discard a trend if:**

1. It's **only** relevant to one specific CMS/platform (e.g. "Shopify-only bug")
2. It's a **PR piece** disguised as news (company announcing their own product as "industry news")
3. The source is **unreliable** and no other sources confirm it
4. It promotes a **competitor** or would require naming one to explain
5. It's **more than 14 days old** with no new developments
6. Covering it would require **speculation** about unconfirmed details
7. The only angle is **fear** ("your website will be sued!") without balanced context
