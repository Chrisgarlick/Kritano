# Audience Personas

## Primary Persona: The Business Owner

**Who they are:**
- Small to medium business owners (1-50 employees)
- Running a service-based or e-commerce business
- Based in the UK, often the North East
- Probably managing their own website or have a small team doing it
- Making decisions on web development, marketing, and digital strategy

**Knowledge level:**
- Non-technical — they know what a website is, but not how it works under the bonnet
- They've heard of SEO, page speed, and accessibility but can't explain them in detail
- Familiar with platforms like WordPress, Wix, Squarespace from a user perspective
- Understand business metrics (leads, conversions, revenue) but not web metrics

**Pain points:**
- "My website looks outdated but I don't know where to start"
- "I'm paying for a website that doesn't bring in any leads"
- "I don't understand why my site is slow"
- "I've been told I need SEO but I don't know what that actually means"
- "Is my website actually accessible? Do I need to care about that?"
- "Should I rebuild or just fix what I've got?"

**What they want to walk away knowing:**
- A clear understanding of the topic in plain English
- Whether this is something they should act on (and how urgently)
- Practical steps they can take themselves or brief a developer on
- Enough knowledge to make informed decisions without being an expert

**How to write for them:**
- Explain every technical term the first time you use it
- Use real-world analogies they'd understand
- Be specific about costs, timeframes, and trade-offs where possible
- Frame everything in terms of business impact (leads, revenue, customer trust)

## Secondary Persona: The Marketing Manager

**Who they are:**
- Marketing professionals at SMEs or agencies
- Responsible for the website as part of their marketing channel mix
- More digitally literate than the business owner, but not a developer
- Reporting to someone on website performance

**Knowledge level:**
- Comfortable with CMS platforms, Google Analytics, basic SEO concepts
- Understands content strategy and user journeys
- Can follow technical explanations but doesn't write code
- Knows the acronyms (CMS, SEO, CTA, UX) but may not know the depth

**Pain points:**
- "How do I make the case for a website rebuild to my boss?"
- "What metrics should I actually be tracking?"
- "Our developer says this will take weeks — is that right?"
- "How do I know if our website is performing well compared to competitors?"

**What they want to walk away knowing:**
- Data-backed insights they can use in reports and pitches
- Best practices they can implement or delegate
- How to evaluate whether their current website is up to standard
- Ammunition for internal conversations about investment

**How to write for them:**
- Slightly more technical language is fine, but still explain niche concepts
- Include statistics and benchmarks where relevant
- Frame advice in terms of what they can action within their role
- Reference specific tools they might already use (GA4, HubSpot, Hotjar)

## Tertiary Persona: The Junior Developer / Freelancer

**Who they are:**
- Early-career developers or freelancers building client websites
- Self-taught or recently trained (relatable to the brand's own story)
- Looking for practical guidance and best practices
- May be choosing between tools, frameworks, or approaches

**Knowledge level:**
- Comfortable with code and technical concepts
- May be deep in one area but shallow in others
- Actively learning and seeking out opinions from experienced practitioners

**Pain points:**
- "What's the best CMS for this type of project?"
- "How should I approach site performance for a client?"
- "What does a proper accessibility audit look like?"
- "How do I explain technical decisions to non-technical clients?"

**What they want to walk away knowing:**
- Practical, experience-backed recommendations (not just theory)
- Real-world examples of what works and what doesn't
- Tools and resources they can use immediately
- How to communicate technical value to clients

**How to write for them:**
- Can use more technical language, but still keep the conversational tone
- Share the "why" behind decisions, not just the "what"
- Reference your own experience and projects as proof points
- Be honest about trade-offs between different approaches

## Persona Selection Guide

| Topic | Primary Audience | Adjust Tone |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Page speed, performance | Business Owner | Plain English, business impact focus |
| CMS comparisons | Business Owner / Junior Dev | Balanced — practical for both |
| SEO fundamentals | Business Owner / Marketing Manager | Accessible but data-aware |
| UX/UI best practices | Marketing Manager | Slightly more sophisticated |
| Accessibility | Business Owner | Emphasise legal/ethical + practical |
| Tool/platform deep-dives | Junior Dev / Marketing Manager | Can go slightly more technical |
| Industry trends | Marketing Manager / Business Owner | Forward-looking, strategic framing |
| Website auditing | All three | Layer the explanation — basics first, depth for those who want it |
