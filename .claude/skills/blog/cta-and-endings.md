# CTAs and Endings

## CTA Style

The brand's CTA style is **soft, inviting, and never pushy**. Think of it as extending an invitation, not issuing a command.

### CTA Language Patterns

**Primary (most common):**
- "If you're looking for help with [topic], get in touch and we can have a chat."
- "Want to see how your website stacks up? Get in touch for a free audit."
- "If you're still unsure, take a look at my work or get in touch."

**Education-first:**
- "If you're wanting a website audit to see how I can help improve your website, please get in touch."
- "Head over to the contact page and fill in the form and I will be in touch soon."

**Portfolio-driven:**
- "Check out my portfolio at [site] or reach out for a chat — I'd love to help."
- "Take a look around and see what I've been working on!"

**Casual/warm:**
- "If you've got any questions, I'm always happy to chat."
- "Drop me a message — I'd love to hear about your project."

### CTA Rules

1. **One CTA per post** — at the end, after delivering value
2. **Never before the content** — earn the right to ask by educating first
3. **Frame as an invitation**, not a demand ("get in touch" not "contact us now")
4. **Reference the topic** — tie the CTA back to what the post covered
5. **No urgency tactics** — no "limited time", "act now", or countdown pressure
6. **No third-person corporate voice** — keep it personal ("I'd love to help" not "our team is standing by")

## Ending Patterns

### Pattern 1: Personal Recommendation + CTA
Best for how-tos and comparisons.

> In my honest opinion, [recommendation with reasoning]. It's not perfect for everyone, but for most [audience], it's the best starting point.
>
> If you're looking for help with [topic], get in touch and we can have a chat about what would work best for you.

### Pattern 2: Summary + Soft Sell
Best for explainers and listicles.

> [Topic] isn't as complicated as it sounds — it's really about [simple summary]. Get the basics right and you'll be ahead of most.
>
> If you want to see how your website measures up, reach out for a free audit and I'll walk you through what I find.

### Pattern 3: Reflection + Invitation
Best for thought leadership and case study narratives.

> This is something I think about a lot, and I've seen it play out across dozens of projects. The businesses that [positive action] are the ones that [positive outcome].
>
> If any of this resonates, I'd love to hear about your project. Drop me a message and let's see what we can do.

### Pattern 4: Quick Takeaway + Link
Best for shorter or more casual posts.

> The bottom line? [One-sentence takeaway].
>
> Take a look at my work or get in touch if you want to chat about it.

---

## What NOT to Do in Endings

- Don't introduce new information in the closing section
- Don't use generic "Thanks for reading!" sign-offs
- Don't link to multiple pages — keep focus on one action
- Don't use exclamation-heavy language ("Don't miss out!!!")
- Don't repeat the entire post in summary form
- Don't end abruptly without a CTA — every post should close the loop
