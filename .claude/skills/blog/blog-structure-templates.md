# Blog Structure Templates

Choose the template that best matches the user's prompt. If no type is specified, default to **how-to** or **explainer**.

---

## 1. How-To Guide

**Best for:** Step-by-step tutorials, practical walkthroughs, "how do I do X?" questions.

**Word count:** 1,200–1,800 words

```
# [How to {Achieve Result} — {Qualifier/Context}]

[Hook: Relatable question or frustration the reader has experienced]
[1-2 sentences on why this matters and what they'll walk away knowing]

## Why {Topic} Matters

[2-3 paragraphs explaining the importance, with a real example or statistic]
[Honest take on common mistakes or misconceptions]

## Step 1: {Action Verb + What}

[Explanation of the step]
[Specific tool or technique recommendation]
[Practical tip or gotcha to watch for]

## Step 2: {Action Verb + What}

[Same pattern — explain, recommend, tip]

## Step 3: {Action Verb + What}

[Same pattern]

[Continue as needed — aim for 4-7 steps]

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

[2-3 pitfalls with brief explanations]

## Wrapping Up

[Personal recommendation or honest opinion]
[Soft CTA — "If you're looking for help with X, get in touch"]
```

## 2. Thought Leadership

**Best for:** Industry opinions, future trends, hot takes, "why X matters" pieces.

**Word count:** 1,000–1,500 words

```
# [Bold Statement or Provocative Question About {Topic}]

[Hook: Observation about the industry or a trend you've noticed]
[Set up the tension — what most people think vs what's actually true]

## The Problem with {Current Approach}

[2-3 paragraphs unpacking why the status quo isn't working]
[Personal experience or example from your own work]

## What I've Seen Work Instead

[Your perspective, backed by experience]
[Name specific tools, approaches, or projects]
[Acknowledge the nuance — "this isn't a silver bullet"]

## Why This Matters Now

[Connect to a broader trend or shift]
[What happens if businesses don't adapt]

## My Take

[Clear, confident opinion — "In my honest opinion..."]
[Practical first step the reader can take today]
[Soft CTA]
```

## 3. Listicle

**Best for:** Tips, tools, mistakes, examples — anything that works as a numbered list.

**Word count:** 1,000–1,600 words

```
# {Number} {Things/Tips/Tools/Mistakes} {for/to/that} {Outcome}

[Hook: Why this list matters — relatable scenario or question]
[Brief context: "I've put together X based on what I've seen work"]

## 1. {Tip/Item Title}

[2-3 sentences explaining why this matters]
[Specific example, tool, or technique]
[Quick practical takeaway]

## 2. {Tip/Item Title}

[Same pattern]

[Continue for all items — aim for 5-10]

## The One That Matters Most

[Pick your top recommendation and explain why]
[Personal opinion: "If I had to choose one..."]
[Soft CTA]
```

## 4. Comparison

**Best for:** X vs Y, platform comparisons, "which should I choose?" questions.

**Word count:** 1,400–2,000 words

```
# {Option A} vs {Option B}: {Which Is Right for You?/An Honest Comparison}

[Hook: The dilemma the reader is facing]
[Set expectations: "I'll break down the pros and cons so you can decide"]

## Quick Overview

[Brief 2-3 sentence summary of each option]

## {Option A}: The Pros

[3-4 genuine advantages with examples]

## {Option A}: The Downsides

[2-3 honest drawbacks — "While I've probably just sold you on..."]

## {Option B}: The Pros

[3-4 genuine advantages with examples]

## {Option B}: The Downsides

[2-3 honest drawbacks]

## So, Which Should You Choose?

[Decision framework: "If you need X, go with A. If you need Y, go with B."]
[Personal recommendation: "But in my honest opinion..."]
[Acknowledge it depends on context]

## My Recommendation

[Clear, confident take]
[Soft CTA]
```

## 5. Case Study Narrative

**Best for:** Project stories, client wins, real-world results.

**Word count:** 1,200–1,800 words

```
# How {Result Was Achieved} for {Client/Project} with {Approach}

[Hook: The challenge or problem that kicked off the project]
[Brief context on who the client is and what they do]

## The Challenge

[Detailed breakdown of the problem — be specific]
[Why existing solutions weren't working]
[What was at stake]

## The Approach

[What was implemented and why]
[Specific tools, platforms, and technologies used]
[Key decisions and trade-offs made along the way]

## The Results

[Measurable outcomes — numbers, improvements, before/after]
[Qualitative wins — client feedback, user experience improvements]

## What I Learned

[Personal reflection on the project]
[What you'd do differently or what surprised you]
[Takeaway the reader can apply to their own work]
[Soft CTA]
```

## 6. Explainer

**Best for:** Breaking down a concept, technology, or trend for non-technical readers.

**Word count:** 1,000–1,500 words

```
# What Is {Concept}? {Subtitle That Promises Clarity}

[Hook: Why the reader should care — how this affects them]
[Promise: "I'll break this down in plain English"]

## {Concept} in Plain English

[Simple, jargon-free explanation — use an analogy if helpful]
[Build up from the basics]

## Why It Matters for Your {Website/Business/Project}

[Practical impact — what happens with it and without it]
[Real example or scenario]

## How It Works (Without the Jargon)

[Step through the mechanics simply]
[Name specific tools or platforms where relevant]
[Use "in essence..." or "think of it like..." for clarity]

## What You Can Do About It

[2-3 actionable steps]
[Specific tools or resources]
[Honest take on difficulty level]

## The Bottom Line

[One-paragraph summary]
[Soft CTA]
```

## Universal Rules (All Post Types)

1. **Opening:** Always a relatable question, scenario, or observation — never start with a definition
2. **Subheadings:** Conversational, sometimes question-based — never dry or academic
3. **Paragraphs:** 3-6 sentences max. Break up with headers, lists, or bold text
4. **Opinions:** At least one "in my honest opinion" or personal take per post
5. **Trade-offs:** Always acknowledge downsides or nuance — never one-sided
6. **CTAs:** Soft and warm. "Get in touch", "take a look", "reach out for a chat"
7. **Tools:** Name at least 2-3 specific tools, platforms, or technologies
8. **Length:** Stay within the word count range for the post type
